Submit a signal

RayTube can invite others without hosting their files.

Other creators would not need to upload huge files to your server at first. They could send a video link from YouTube, Odysee, PeerTube, or another public video host. RayTube could then embed approved videos into curated pages.

  • Creator sends title, description, video link, thumbnail, and permission to embed.
  • You approve what fits RayTube.
  • The video appears on RayTube inside the right broadcast channel.
  • The original host still handles streaming and bandwidth.

About RayTube

A friendly archive front door.

RayTube.video is an independent broadcast archive built to give creative videos a home outside the usual platform noise. It starts as a single editable HTML page with embedded videos, keeping it light, affordable, and easy to update through GitHub. Over time it can grow into a curated video hub, a creator showcase, or eventually a self-hosted video platform if the archive needs its own engine.

The name RayTube comes from James’s love of old screens and broadcast technology: cathode-ray tube televisions, chunky CRT monitors, glowing glass, scanlines, phosphor light, and the warm feeling of retro machines with worlds inside them. “Ray” carries the sense of light, signal, beam, and transmission. “Tube” nods to both the old cathode-ray tube and modern video culture. Together, RayTube means old television soul meeting current and future video technology: a friendly signal box for independent broadcasts.
